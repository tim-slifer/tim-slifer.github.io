Hi, my name is Tim. I'm an Automation Engineer, and this is my blog...

<!--more-->

I've worked in the QA field since 2002, have been involved in numerous automation efforts along the way, and have 
focused exclusively on automated testing with Selenium since 2012. During my transition into a more development 
oriented role, my focus and primary goal has been to leverage my skills in a way that can make the working lives of 
my fellow testers just a bit easier. In my work, this equates not only to putting the right tools, process, and 
people into place, but getting outside the box to help solve the testing challenges presented by an application. As 
well, I enjoy the opportunity to pay it forward to everyone who has ever brought me forward in my career, and mentor 
those on my team by helping to enhance their skill sets and mature as a true engineer in test.

My plans for this site are basically to store a bit of know-how, and as a means to meet and network with other 
like-minded engineers in the QA space. My hope is to engage, share ideas, experiences of what has and has not worked 
in terms of approach, and even learn something new. 

I plan to add new content here on at least a semi-regular basis. I'll showcase some of my personal projects from time 
to time, including proofs of concept and open source tools. As well, I'll post some tutorials and HOWTOs for working 
through various automation challenges. Beyond that? Let's see where this takes us.
